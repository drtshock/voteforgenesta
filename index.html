WHY I'M RUNNING

I'm running for Student Senate President because I believe every student deserves meaningful representation in the decisions that shape our academic experience.

I want to build a Student Senate that students actually feel connected to — one built on transparent communication, proactive advocacy, and leadership that goes out of its way to include every voice on campus.

I believe student government works best when it's rooted in active listening, strategic planning, and genuine commitment to our community. I'm ready to show up and do the work.

MY PLATFORM

Five areas where I'll show up, advocate hard, and move the needle — incrementally and intentionally — for every student at Saint Paul College.

01

FOOD, NUTRITION & BASIC NEEDS

Students can't learn on an empty stomach or on cafeteria food that doesn't reflect who they are. I'll push for real improvements and make sure every student can access the support they need.

  • Healthier, fresh, and locally sourced cafeteria options
  • Halal and Kosher menu options as a standard offering
  • More affordable meal pricing for students
  • Expanded access to basic needs support services and grant funding
REAL FOOD, REAL SUPPORT
02

STUDENT RIGHTS & PROTECTIONS

Every student deserves to know — and actually access — the full protections available to them. I'll make sure the Senate actively communicates and advocates for students' rights.

  • Clearer communication about Title IX and federal protections on campus
  • Proactive outreach about rights and exceptions relevant to the current political climate
  • Improved accommodations and accessibility for students with disabilities
KNOW YOUR RIGHTS
03

ACADEMIC SUPPORT & TUTORING

Struggling students shouldn't have to figure it out alone. I'll build out robust support systems so every student has someone in their corner.

  • Expand existing tutoring services and hours
  • Create a peer tutoring program connecting students across subjects
  • Launch a faculty tutoring initiative to bridge classroom and office hours
SUPPORT THAT SHOWS UP
04

CHILDCARE & TRANSPORTATION

Getting to campus — and staying there — shouldn't be a luxury. I'll work to remove the logistical barriers that push student parents and commuters out the door.

  • Access to free on-campus childcare during study time for student parents
  • Transportation supports: free or subsidized rideshare rides
  • Gas grants and transit assistance for commuting students
GET HERE. STAY HERE.
05

COMMUNITY & BELONGING

Commuter campuses can feel like a bus stop. I want Saint Paul College to feel like a place you actually want to be — welcoming, alive, and built for all of us.

  • More campus-funded events with real community and cultural representation
  • Low or no-cost community meals and gatherings
  • Better, more inviting spaces to study, socialize, and decompress between classes
BUILD THE VIBE

QUESTIONS? ANSWERS.

CAN STUDENT SENATE ACTUALLY CHANGE THE FOOD?

Yes. Student Senate has a direct advisory role to administration on student services, including dining. Senate recommendations on menu changes and vendor contracts carry real weight — especially when backed by student survey data, which I plan to collect in my first month.

WHAT'S THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN SENATE PRESIDENT AND OTHER SENATE ROLES?

The President chairs every General Assembly and Executive Board meeting, prepares the agendas, and represents Saint Paul College at all Minnesota State College Student Association (MSCSA) conferences. They coordinate directly with the college President and the VP of Finance & Operations on tuition and fee decisions. Other Senate roles support specific areas, but the President is the primary voice between students and the people who make institutional decisions.

I'VE NEVER VOTED IN A CAMPUS ELECTION. DOES MY VOTE MATTER?

Student Senate elections are often decided by a few dozen votes. Your single vote has more impact here than in almost any other election you'll participate in. This is the closest thing to direct democracy you'll experience — use it.

HOW DO I KNOW THESE PROMISES ARE REALISTIC?

Every item on my platform maps to something Student Senate has the authority to influence — through committee recommendations, budget allocation, or direct advocacy to administration. I'm not promising to build a new building. I'm promising to show up to every meeting, bring real data, and push until something moves.

I DON'T KNOW ANYTHING ABOUT STUDENT GOVERNMENT. IS THAT OK?

That's exactly who I'm building this campaign for. Student government works best when every student feels like it belongs to them — not just the students who already know how it works. Start here: read the platform, come to the townhall on March 4, and vote March 9–13.
